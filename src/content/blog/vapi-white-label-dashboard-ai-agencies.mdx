faq:
  - question: "Can you white label Vapi?"
    answer: "Vapi does not offer a white-label version of its dashboard for agencies. You connect Vapi's API to a white-label portal platform like Getflowetic, VoiceAIWrapper, Vapify, Voicerr, or ChatDash. The client sees your brand. Vapi's own staff has acknowledged this gap and points to third-party solutions in their community forum."
  - question: "What is a Vapi white label dashboard?"
    answer: "It's a branded client portal showing your AI voice agent results under your agency's brand. The dashboard displays call volume, success rates, cost per call, and transcripts. Your client logs in and sees their data, your logo, your colors. No Vapi branding is visible anywhere."
  - question: "How do agencies charge for Vapi voice agent services?"
    answer: "Most charge $500 to $3,000 per month depending on call volume and complexity. The typical model combines a monthly portal fee ($99 to $299) with a per-minute markup. Vapi's real production cost is $0.13 to $0.33 per minute. Agencies bill clients $0.25 to $0.50 per minute and keep the margin."
  - question: "How long does it take to set up a white-label Vapi client portal?"
    answer: "Under 60 seconds for repeat setups on most platforms once your Vapi API key is connected. First-time setup with branding and Stripe takes 10 to 30 minutes. A custom build takes weeks to months and costs $10,000 or more."
  - question: "What metrics should I show clients in a Vapi dashboard?"
    answer: "Call volume, success rate, average call duration, cost per call vs. human alternative, and searchable transcripts. The most effective portals translate raw data into ROI, showing estimated savings rather than raw numbers."
  - question: "Do I need to give clients access to my Vapi account?"
    answer: "No, and you shouldn't. Giving clients your Vapi login exposes your API keys, assistant configurations, and per-minute costs. A dashboard layer shows them only what they need to see while protecting your business."
