faq:
  - question: "Does Make.com have a white label option for agencies?"
    answer: "Make.com has an OEM White Label program, but it's designed for enterprise software companies embedding Make into their own products. It requires direct sales contact, custom pricing, and is not accessible to small or mid-size automation agencies. There's no self-serve white-label plan."
  - question: "What is Make.com's white label pricing?"
    answer: "Make.com doesn't publish OEM white-label pricing. It's quote-based and requires a direct sales conversation. Standard Make plans range from free to $34.12/month for Teams, with Enterprise pricing on request. The white-label tier sits above all of these."
  - question: "Can you make money reselling Make.com automations?"
    answer: "Yes. Agencies build automations in Make and charge clients $199 to $500+ per month for the managed service plus a branded dashboard. The key is never giving clients direct Make access. Instead, wrap the output in a branded client portal with Stripe billing so clients pay you, not Make."

  - question: "How do you make money with white label automation apps?"
    answer: "Build the automation once in Make or n8n. Wrap it in a branded client portal with a monthly fee. Charge clients $149 to $499/month for dashboard access showing what their automations are doing. Your cost is the Make plan plus the portal tool. Everything above that is margin."
  - question: "Does Make.com charge per operation when reselling to clients?"
    answer: "Yes. Make's pricing is credit-based, and every module action in a scenario costs one credit. If you're running client workflows on your Make account, you absorb the operation costs. This is why the margin math matters. Your client fee needs to cover Make's per-operation cost plus your profit."
