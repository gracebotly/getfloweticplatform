## Why automation agencies need a white-label dashboard

Let's be honest about what actually happens without one.

You built a Vapi voice receptionist that handles 400 calls a month for a dental
practice. Your client has no idea. They see a lower call volume at the front
desk and assume things are slow. They cancel your retainer after 90 days.

You built an n8n workflow that qualifies and enriches leads before they hit the
CRM. Your client just sees cleaner data. They think that is normal. They do not
renew.

This is where agencies lose recurring revenue — not because the work was not
there, but because the proof was not visible. A white-label dashboard turns your
invisible backend into something your client opens every Monday morning. They
see call volumes, workflow runs, cost savings, and ROI trends — all under your
brand. That is what makes them stay. And it is what makes them pay monthly for
access.

## What should a white-label AI client dashboard include?

Most platforms get one or two of these right. Demand all five before you commit.

**1. Complete white-labeling — not partial.** Your domain, your logo, your
colors. Zero mention of the underlying platform anywhere. Partial
white-labeling — where your brand shows but the platform name still appears in
footers, emails, or subdomains — is worse than none. It makes your clients
question whether they are paying you or someone else.

**2. Real-time platform data.** For voice agencies: call volume, success rates,
sentiment, and transcripts pulled live from Vapi or Retell. For workflow
agencies: execution counts, failure rates, and runtime trends from Make or n8n.
A dashboard that pulls weekly CSV exports and slaps a logo on them is not a
client portal. It is a spreadsheet in disguise.

**3. ROI metrics your client actually understands.** "Your AI receptionist
handled 847 calls last month and saved an estimated $12,400 in staff costs" is
a renewal conversation. A raw call log is not. The platform should translate
data into business outcomes automatically — or you will be building that
translation layer yourself every month.

**4. Stripe billing built in.** If you want to charge clients monthly for
portal access, the platform must handle payments so clients pay you directly.
You should not be invoicing manually. You should not be building a checkout
page.

**5. Setup under 10 minutes without a developer.** If standing up a new client
portal requires engineering time, the platform is not built for agencies. The
setup should be a wizard, not a ticket.

### Stammer.ai

Stammer is one of the most recognized names in white-label AI, with over 1,300
agency customers. But it is frequently misunderstood — and that confusion costs
agencies months of setup time.

**What it actually is:** A platform for building and reselling AI chat and
voice agents under your own brand. Stammer creates the agents. You brand and
resell them to clients.

**Pricing:** Agency plan at $197/month (20 agents), Full SaaS Mode at
$497/month (unlimited agents). Both billed quarterly.

**What it does well:** Agent creation, branded agent delivery, client
sub-accounts, and internal billing for agent subscriptions. Its onboarding
documentation and community support are genuinely strong. If you want to launch
an AI agent reselling business from zero, Stammer is a fast path.

**What it does not do:** Stammer cannot generate a passthrough analytics
dashboard for agents you already built on Vapi, Retell, Make, or n8n. It runs
on its own infrastructure. If your stack is already built elsewhere, Stammer is
not a dashboard layer — it is a completely different product solving a different
problem.

**Best for:** Agencies that want to build and resell agents from scratch and do
not have an existing Vapi, Retell, Make, or n8n stack.

Custom development looks cheaper until you run the real numbers.

A realistic custom build costs $30,000–$80,000 in initial development. Ongoing
maintenance runs $500–$2,000/month. When Vapi, Retell, or Make updates their
API — which all three do regularly — your dashboard breaks and a developer has
to fix it. At 5 engineer hours per breakage event, those incidents add up fast.

At $99/month, Getflowetic pays for itself the moment one client pays $149/month
for portal access. The custom build does not pay for itself until you are at

right now.

Custom builds make sense for agencies with 50+ clients, specific requirements
no platform addresses, and a developer on staff. For everyone else, it is a
trap that delays recurring revenue by three to six months.
